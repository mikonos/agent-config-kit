# Confirmation Filters

Filters adjust confidence, waiting discipline, or position size. They do not define Chan structure.

## MACD

Use only after naming compared segments.

Check:

- Histogram area in movement direction.
- DIF/DEA position relative to zero axis.
- Bar length change near new high/low.
- Whether the lower-level center is broken after divergence.

Interpretation:

- Trend divergence: compare final segment against prior same-direction segment after center.
- Consolidation divergence: compare same-direction oscillation legs around one center.
- MACD divergence without structure is only a warning.

## RSI

Use as strength/overextension confirmation:

- Low RSI with structural first buy can support probe.
- RSI recovery during second buy pullback supports durability.
- High RSI with first sell supports risk reduction.
- RSI below 50 during rebound supports second sell.

Do not define Chan buy/sell points with RSI alone.

## Moving Averages And Trendlines

Useful confluence:

- MA100/MA120/MA250 near center boundary can support boundary quality.
- Uptrend line near center lower bound supports low-risk observation.
- Downtrend line near center upper bound supports sell-side caution.

Failure:

- A MA break without structural destruction is not enough.
- A structure invalidation should override MA hope.

## Volume, Turnover, Money Flow

For A-share/TuShare work, read `volume-turnover-money.md`.

Core principle:

- Volume/turnover/money flow explain whether the structural point is being accepted or rejected by market participation.
- They are strongest for grading second buys, third buys, first sells, and second sells.

## Chips And Floating Supply

Optional and data-dependent:

- Chip peak near lower boundary can support a buy.
- Chip peak near upper boundary can create pressure.
- High floating-supply pressure reduces breakout confidence.

If no reliable chip data exists, skip rather than invent.

## Sector Strength

Useful for A-share practice:

- In broad bull phases, prioritize valid structures in stronger sectors.
- In weak markets, sell-side signals and failed third buys deserve more weight.
- Sector strength cannot override invalidated structure.

## Filter Output

Classify filters as:

- `confirm`: supports structure.
- `neutral`: no strong evidence.
- `caution`: lowers position or waits for lower-level trigger.
- `veto`: rejects action despite structural candidate.
