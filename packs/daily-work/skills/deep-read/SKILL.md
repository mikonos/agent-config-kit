# Deep Read

Work from the exact source or full accessible text. If the source is missing or unreadable, say what is unavailable instead of inventing its contents.

## Read in layers

1. Identify the central question and thesis.
2. Reconstruct the argument: claims, evidence, method, examples, and conclusions.
3. Mark what is source evidence, author interpretation, your inference, and an open question.
4. Find the strongest counterpoint, limitation, or missing premise.
5. Explain the hardest idea in plain language with one concrete example.

## Default output

Give the thesis, argument map, key evidence, counterpoint, surprising implication, unresolved questions, and practical use. Keep quotations short and preserve the source's uncertainty.
