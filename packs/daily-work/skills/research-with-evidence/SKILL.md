# Research With Evidence

Turn the request into an answerable decision or question before searching.

## Workflow

1. List the few claims that must be true for the answer to hold.
2. Prefer primary and authoritative sources; add independent secondary sources when interpretation or disagreement matters.
3. Check dates, scope, definitions, and whether a source actually supports the claim.
4. Separate verified facts, source interpretations, your inferences, and recommendations.
5. Look for a counterexample or disconfirming source before concluding.
6. Cite the exact pages used and state remaining uncertainty.

Never treat search snippets as evidence. Do not hide conflicting evidence or turn a weak source into a confident claim.
